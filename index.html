Permission comes before the method.

Use Whitehack only on systems you own or have explicit permission to assess. The framework does not grant authorization. Keep credentials, personal data, and unresolved exploit details out of public cases.

Read the safety boundary
One inspectable chain

From intention to evidence.

Each link names a question. Missing links become visible work—not optimistic prose hidden in a report.

  1. 01

    Intent

    What is this system for, and what does it explicitly not promise?

  2. 02

    Invariant

    What must remain true for that intention to survive contact with reality?

  3. 03

    Threat

    Who can falsify the invariant, through which trust boundary?

  4. 04

    Control

    What design choice intends to prevent, detect, correct, or recover?

  5. 05

    Implementation

    Where does that control exist in code, configuration, or process?

  6. 06

    Evidence

    What observed result supports the claim—and when was it observed?

  7. 07

    Residual risk

    What remains, who owns it, and what event makes the decision change?

No build. No account. Standard library only.

Your first case is one JSON file.

Copy the template, replace the starter content, then validate the references and render a compact Markdown dashboard.

terminal 
$ cp integrity/templates/integrity-case.json \
    my-system.integrity.json

$ python3 integrity/integrity_case.py validate \
    my-system.integrity.json

OK 4/4 invariants supported

$ python3 integrity/integrity_case.py render \
    my-system.integrity.json --output dashboard.md
